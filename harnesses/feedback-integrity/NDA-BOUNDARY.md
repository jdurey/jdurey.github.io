# NDA Boundary

This harness exists because of a real engagement I can't show you — a large bank of AI-authored K-8
curriculum items under NDA. So let me be exact about what's mine to publish and what isn't.

## What's public (this repo)

- **The method.** The three-class taxonomy (answer-reveal, ghost-distractor, feedback-option
  mismatch) plus the structural length cue, the cheapest-rung-first pipeline, the blind-solver
  exploitability test, the trusted-rung-vs-cheap-proposer split, and the per-class graduation
  scorecard (recall, noise tripwire, canary battery, READY/HOLD). Method is portable; I restated it
  from first principles and here it is, running.
- **A synthetic, fictional bank built from scratch.** Invented peoples, places, goods, and
  institutions. The items, the keys, the feedback, and the golden labels were authored for this
  demo. They share nothing with any client's bank, items, or examples.
- **Real local-model runs** against that synthetic bank, with every raw response saved.

## What's private (and stays that way)

- **The client and the program.** Not named, not hinted, not reconstructable from anything here. The
  synthetic bank was deliberately built to be *structurally* analogous (AI-authored MCQ feedback at
  K-8 grade bands) while being *substantively* unrelated.
- **Any client item, key, feedback string, rubric, count, or catch rate.** None of it was copied,
  paraphrased, de-identified, or used as a template for the synthetic version. The synthetic
  material was generated independently, from a fictional world. The real numbers are not quoted.
- **The human reviewers.** In the real engagement the adjudicator is a human (or a strong model
  under human sign-off). Nothing about that work appears here; the public demo fills the adjudicator
  slot with a labeled golden findings file instead.

## The line, in one sentence

I'm showing you the **instrument**, not the **bank**. If you want it pointed at *your* items and
*your* standard, that's the [transfer memo](TRANSFER-MEMO.md) — and it runs on your data under your
NDA, exactly as the original did.
