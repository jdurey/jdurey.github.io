## SECTION I — PERILS INSURED AGAINST

We insure against direct physical loss to covered property caused by the following
perils, unless the loss is excluded in Section I — Exclusions:

1. **Fire or smoke.**
2. **Windstorm or hail.**
3. **Theft**, including attempted theft and loss of property from a known place when
   it is likely the property has been stolen.
4. **Sudden and accidental discharge or overflow of water or steam** from within a
   plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, or household appliance system.
5. **Falling objects.**
6. **Vandalism or malicious mischief.**

Any peril not named above is not covered.

## SECTION I — EXCLUSIONS

We do **not** insure for loss caused directly or indirectly by any of the following.
**Such loss is excluded regardless of any other cause or event contributing
concurrently or in any sequence to the loss** (this is the "anti-concurrent
causation" provision):

a. **Water damage**, meaning flood, surface water, waves, tidal water, storm surge,
   overflow of a body of water, or spray from any of these, whether or not driven by
   wind.

b. **Earth movement**, meaning earthquake, landslide, mudflow, sinkhole, or any other
   movement of earth.

c. **Wear and tear**, marring, deterioration, rust, corrosion, latent defect, or any
   quality in property that causes it to damage or destroy itself; and **faulty,
   inadequate, or defective maintenance**.

d. **Mold, fungus, wet or dry rot.**

e. **Intentional loss**, meaning any loss arising out of an act committed by or at the
   direction of an insured with the intent to cause a loss; and any **fraudulent** act
   in connection with the claim.

f. **Constant or repeated seepage or leakage** of water or steam over a period of
   **fourteen days or more**.

g. **Vacancy.** We do not pay for any loss occurring while the dwelling has been
   **vacant for more than 60 consecutive days** immediately before the loss.

### Ensuing loss

If an excluded cause of loss listed in **(c) wear and tear** or **(d) mold** results
in a **separate ensuing loss by a peril we do insure against**, we cover that ensuing
loss — but **not** the cost to repair or replace the worn, defective, or moldy
property that was the original excluded cause. The ensuing-loss provision applies
**only** to (c) and (d). It does **not** restore coverage for loss that ensues from
flood, earth movement, intentional acts, seepage, or vacancy.

## SECTION I — CONDITIONS

**Deductible.** In any one loss we pay only that part of the covered loss that exceeds
the deductible shown below. We apply the deductible **after** any sub-limit.

- Standard deductible: **$1,000**.
- Windstorm or hail deductible: **$2,500**. This higher deductible applies whenever the
  proximate covered peril is windstorm or hail.

**Special limits of liability (sub-limits).** Our limit for loss by the perils insured
against is reduced for the following categories. The sub-limit is the most we pay for
that category in any one loss, and it is applied **before** the deductible:

- **$1,500** on jewelry, watches, and furs — **for loss by theft only**.
- **$2,500** on firearms — **for loss by theft only**.
- **$2,500** on electronics.
- **$2,500** on business property while on the residence premises.
- **$200** on money, bank notes, and cash.

(For jewelry and firearms, a loss by a covered peril *other than theft* — e.g., fire —
is **not** subject to these sub-limits.)

**Determination of cause.** Where the proximate cause of a loss cannot be reasonably
determined from the facts presented — for example, where the evidence is equally
consistent with a covered peril and with an excluded peril — the claim is **referred
for investigation** before any coverage decision is made. We do not approve or deny on
an assumption of cause.
