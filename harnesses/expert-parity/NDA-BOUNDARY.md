# NDA Boundary

This artifact exists because of a real engagement I can't show you — so let me be exact about
what's mine to publish and what isn't.

## What's public (this repo)

- **The method.** Domain distillation, the structured-core schema pattern, the blind A/B
  protocol, the metrics layer (κ, bootstrap CIs, false-pay/false-deny, subgroup slices), the
  adversarial-hardening loop, and the audit trail. Method is portable; I can restate it from
  first principles, and here I have.
- **A synthetic domain built from scratch.** "Meridian Mutual" property insurance is fictional.
  The policy, the claims, the rules, and the ground truth were authored for this demo. They
  share nothing with any client's domain, data, or examples.
- **Real model runs** against that synthetic domain, with every raw response saved.

## What's private (and stays that way)

- **The client and the domain** of the original engagement. Not named, not hinted, not
  reconstructable from anything here. The synthetic domain was deliberately chosen to be
  *structurally* analogous (messy inputs → structured core → blind parity proof) while being
  *substantively* unrelated.
- **Any client document, dataset, rubric, example, or result.** None of it was copied,
  summarized, paraphrased, or used as a template for the synthetic version. The synthetic
  material was generated independently.
- **The human expert.** In the real engagement the reference standard was a credentialed human.
  Nothing about that person or their judgments appears here; the public demo simulates the
  examiner slot with a deterministic engine instead.

## The line, in one sentence

I'm showing you the **instrument**, not the **case file**. If you want the instrument pointed at
*your* model and *your* expert, that's the [transfer memo](TRANSFER-MEMO.md) — and it runs on
your data under your NDA, exactly as the original did.
